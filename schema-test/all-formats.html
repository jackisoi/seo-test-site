FAQ (Microdata — partial markup)

What is structured data?

Structured data is code in a specific format that helps search engines understand your page content. It uses vocabulary from schema.org.

What are the three formats for structured data?

JSON-LD (recommended by Google), Microdata (HTML attributes), and RDFa (similar to Microdata).

Can you mix formats on one page?

Yes, you can use JSON-LD, Microdata, and RDFa on the same page. Google can read all three.

How do I validate my structured data?

Use Google's Rich Results Test or Schema.org Validator to check your markup.

Is structured data a ranking factor?

Not directly, but it can lead to rich results which improve CTR and visibility.
